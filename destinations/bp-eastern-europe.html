Eastern Europe

Prague, Budapest, Kraków, Warsaw — a circuit of extraordinary cities that cost half what their western counterparts do.

Europe

Why backpack Eastern Europe?

Eastern Europe is the backpacker's European sweet spot — all the culture and architecture of the west at half the price. Prague has the most beautiful old town in Europe. Budapest has the thermal baths and the Danube. Kraków has the heartbreaking history of Auschwitz and the extraordinary salt mines. And Warsaw is a city rebuilt from rubble with a resilience that's impossible not to admire.

The Highlights

8 Things to Do in Eastern Europe

01
Get lost in Prague's old town at night when the crowds have gone
02
Take the boat along the Danube between Bratislava and Budapest
03
Soak in Budapest's Széchenyi thermal baths at midnight
04
Visit Auschwitz-Birkenau from Kraków — essential, devastating, important
05
Descend into the Wieliczka Salt Mine near Kraków — an underground cathedral
06
Take the night train from Prague to Warsaw or Budapest
07
Eat chimney cakes, pierogi, langos, and trdelník from market stalls
08
Explore Ceský Krumlov — a castle town so pretty it barely seems real
