My Story

A university student who just wants to drop everything

I started this while juggling lectures and deadlines, because I kept thinking — there has to be more to life than this. Not in a dramatic way. Just in a there's an entire world out there and I want to actually see it kind of way.

Drop Everything And Go began as a personal obsession with finding out what places are really like — not the filtered Instagram version, but the street food stalls, the local guesthouses, the buses that break down in the middle of nowhere, the conversations with people who've lived there their whole lives. That's the travel I care about.

I'm building this start-up because I genuinely believe that travel doesn't have to be expensive, sanitised, or handed to you by a massive package holiday company. It can be raw, affordable, and life-changing — if you know how to do it right.

I'm passionate about eco-travel and supporting local communities. Every guide I write tries to point you towards locally-owned stays, ethical experiences, and ways to leave a place better than you found it. That's not a marketing line — it's just how I think travel should work.

What I Stand For

My Values

🌿

Eco-Travel First

Every guide prioritises low-impact travel — locally-owned stays, overland routes over unnecessary flights, and experiences that fund conservation rather than undermine it.

🏘️

Support Local Communities

Your money matters. I actively steer away from international chains towards family guesthouses, local guides, and markets that put money directly into the hands of people who live there.

🎒

Real Culture Over Tourism

Skip the tourist traps. I'm obsessed with finding the places locals actually go — the food they eat, the routes they take, the parts of their country that never make it into the brochure.
